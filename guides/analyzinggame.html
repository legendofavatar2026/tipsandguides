Damage Formula & Optimization Guide

Updated July 2026

📖 Introduction

This guide summarizes community testing on the Legend of Avatar damage calculation system. The formulas and recommendations below were derived through extensive in-game testing and experimentation rather than official developer documentation.

Understanding how different damage multipliers interact allows you to prioritize upgrades that provide the highest real damage increase instead of simply increasing displayed stats.

⚙️ Base Damage Multipliers

The game uses several base multipliers before additional bonuses are applied.

  • Normal Attack: 100% Attack Power
  • Double Attack: 100% of Normal Attack
  • Triple Attack: 150% of Double Attack
  • Critical Damage: 150% of Non-Critical Damage

⚔️ Double & Triple Damage Formula

Double Damage

Double Damage = Normal Attack Damage × Double Attack Multiplier

Triple Damage

Triple Damage = Double Attack Damage × Triple Attack Multiplier

Example

If a normal attack deals 100 damage:

  • Double Attack = 50 × 2 = 100
  • Triple Attack = 50 × 3 = 150

📊 Damage Multiplier Formula

Every damage category belongs to a separate multiplier group.

Final Damage Formula

  • OO Damage
  • OO Bonus Damage
  • OO Final Damage
  • OO Ultimate Damage
  • OO Damage Increase Buffs (Pets, Skills, Relics, Wings)

Important: Each damage group multiplies with the others. However, values inside the same group are added together.

This means increasing every multiplier group evenly produces much higher damage than stacking only one type of bonus.

✅ TIP 1 — Accessory Upgrade Priority

Upgrade Boss Damage Only

Do not prioritize Attack, Defense, or HP on accessories. Instead, invest all accessory upgrades into Boss Damage.

Why?

Boss Damage has very few sources in the game:

  • Relic (approximately 28%)
  • Accessories (primary source)

Since the Boss Damage multiplier has little competition, every accessory upgrade directly increases your real DPS.

Example (Level 189)

  • Boss Damage ≈ +190%
  • Real Efficiency ≈ 290%

By comparison, Attack upgrades appear much larger but belong to an already overcrowded additive group.

Attack Sources

  • Mythic Transformations ≈ 15,000%
  • Skill Codex ≈ 7,000%
  • Pet Codex ≈ 7,000%
  • Artifacts ≈ 4,600%
  • Goblin Bonuses ≈ 10,000%

Total additive Attack bonuses exceed 39,000%. An additional 1,500% Attack only provides roughly 3–4% real damage gain, making it significantly weaker than Boss Damage investment.

✅ TIP 2 — Artifact Selection

Choosing between the Normal Attack Damage +50 artifact and the Boss Damage +80 artifact depends on your existing Boss Damage.

Situation Choose Normal Damage
No Executioner & No Shadow Thorn Boss Damage exceeds 60%
Executioner OR Shadow Thorn Boss Damage exceeds 108%
Executioner + Shadow Thorn Boss Damage exceeds 156%

Below these values, the Boss Damage artifact remains stronger. As accessory levels increase, the Normal Damage artifact gradually becomes the better long-term option.

✅ TIP 3 — Best Pet Choice

Pet Comparison

Pet Bonus Estimated Efficiency Recommendation
Attack Speed +65% ≈39% Good early, loses value near attack speed cap.
Double Damage +60% ≈35–44% Best Overall Choice
Boss Damage +54% ≈21% Lowest average efficiency.

Attack Speed Pet

  • Base Attack Speed ≈ 180%
  • Attack Speed Cap = 250%
  • Additional Attack Speed loses value as you approach the cap.
  • Acceleration buffs reduce its effectiveness even further.

Double Damage Pet

The Double Damage bonus stacks additively with Halo and Executioner.

  • With Halo + Executioner: approximately 35% efficiency
  • Without Executioner: approximately 44% efficiency

This makes the Double Damage pet the strongest overall option for most builds.

Boss Damage Pet

  • Boss Damage stacks with Snipe.
  • With Snipe active: roughly 14% efficiency.
  • Without Snipe: around 54% efficiency.
  • Average uptime results in approximately 21% overall efficiency.

Overall, the Boss Damage pet provides the weakest average damage increase among the three choices.

✅ TIP 5 — Best Relics from Alchemy Traces

Alchemy Traces are limited, so spending them efficiently is important.

Recommended Priority

  1. Strength
  2. Shadow Thorn
  3. Blaze Fury
  4. Frost Fang
  5. Grim Reaper / Evil Moon
  6. Durandal

Strength

  • +36.2% Attack at Level 5
  • Approximately 60% uptime
  • ≈22% real efficiency
  • Consumes one skill slot

Despite using a skill slot, Strength performs similarly to Shadow Thorn in overall effectiveness.

Shadow Thorn

  • Normal Damage +30
  • ≈17–20% efficiency
  • Value depends on whether Executioner is owned.

Blaze Fury

  • Boss Damage +30
  • ≈18% efficiency with lower accessory levels
  • Drops to roughly 10% at very high accessory levels

Frost Fang

  • Double Damage +30
  • Shares the same additive group as Specialization, Elysia, and Artifact bonuses
  • Approximately 3% efficiency

Grim Reaper & Evil Moon

  • Attack Speed +6
  • ≈3% efficiency
  • Provides a slight PvP timing advantage

Durandal

  • Boss Damage Reduction +10
  • Excellent defensive scaling
  • At roughly 55% Boss Damage Reduction, another 10% reduces incoming damage by approximately 28%

Durandal is recommended after obtaining the primary offensive relics.

✅ TIP 7 — Best Forge Options

For maximum damage efficiency, prioritize forge rolls that improve multipliers with the fewest competing sources.

Recommended Setup

  • Final Double Damage ×3
  • Bonus Boss Damage ×2

Why This Works

  • Final Double Damage sources total roughly 556%
  • Each forge roll adds around 60%
  • Approximately 10.8% efficiency per roll before diminishing returns

Bonus Boss Damage is largely exclusive to Forge, meaning every roll contributes close to its full value.

After several rolls, diminishing returns begin to reduce efficiency, making a 3 Final Double Damage + 2 Bonus Boss Damage setup the most balanced choice.

Critical Damage becomes even more valuable when additional bonuses from the Transformation Codex are unlocked, increasing the effectiveness of every Critical Damage line on your equipment.

💡 Final Recommendations

  • Prioritize upgrades that belong to unique multiplier groups rather than overcrowded additive groups.
  • Boss Damage remains one of the strongest long-term investments because relatively few systems increase it.
  • Avoid judging upgrades by their displayed percentages alone—real efficiency depends on how each bonus interacts with the game's damage formula.
  • Balance your multiplier groups whenever possible to maximize multiplicative scaling.
  • As your account progresses, periodically reevaluate relics, artifacts, pets, and forge options since optimal choices can change as additive bonuses increase.

Following these principles will help maximize your damage output while avoiding investments with poor long-term efficiency.
